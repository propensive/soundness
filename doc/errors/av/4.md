# `SN-av/4` — `TzdbError`

Raised when Aviation cannot parse a timezone database (TZDB) file.

## Cause

`TzdbError` is thrown while reading the IANA TZDB. Each variant
identifies the kind of corruption or unexpected content. The
message reports the line number: `the timezone could not be parsed
at line $line: $reason`.

## Variants

### `SN-av/4.1` — `CouldNotParseTime`

A time value on the line did not parse.

**Example.** `[↯SN-av/4.1] the timezone could not be parsed at line 42: could not parse time 25:00`

**Resolution.** Repair the source TZDB file or use a different copy.

### `SN-av/4.2` — `UnexpectedRule`

A `Rule` line appeared where the parser was not expecting one.

**Example.** `[↯SN-av/4.2] the timezone could not be parsed at line 42: unexpected rule`

**Resolution.** Confirm the file follows the documented TZDB
structure.

### `SN-av/4.3` — `UnexpectedLink`

A `Link` line appeared in an unexpected position.

**Example.** `[↯SN-av/4.3] the timezone could not be parsed at line 42: unexpected link`

**Resolution.** Confirm the file follows the documented TZDB
structure.

### `SN-av/4.4` — `UnexpectedZoneInfo`

A zone-info line appeared where it shouldn't.

**Example.** `[↯SN-av/4.4] the timezone could not be parsed at line 42: unexpected zone info`

**Resolution.** Confirm the file follows the documented TZDB
structure.

### `SN-av/4.5` — `UnparsableDate`

A date field on the line could not be parsed.

**Example.** `[↯SN-av/4.5] the timezone could not be parsed at line 42: the date could not be parsed`

**Resolution.** Repair the offending line.

### `SN-av/4.6` — `BadZoneInfo`

A zone-info line had structurally invalid content.

**Example.** `[↯SN-av/4.6] the timezone could not be parsed at line 42: bad zone information: [ Foo   Bar   Baz ]`

**Resolution.** Confirm the line has the correct number and order of
columns.

### `SN-av/4.7` — `BadName`

A name (zone or rule) was syntactically invalid.

**Example.** `[↯SN-av/4.7] the timezone could not be parsed at line 42: the name foo/Bar! is not valid`

**Resolution.** Use only the characters allowed by the TZDB syntax.

### `SN-av/4.8` — `NoTzdbFile`

The named TZDB file could not be located on the classpath.

**Example.** `[↯SN-av/4.8] the timezone could not be parsed at line 0: the zonefile europe could not be found on the classpath`

**Resolution.** Ensure the TZDB resources are bundled on the
classpath; the Aviation distribution provides them.
